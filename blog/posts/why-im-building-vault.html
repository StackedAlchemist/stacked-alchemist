I don't talk about this stuff much. But I'm building something that matters and I think it's time to be real about why.

I lost my son at 37 weeks. No heartbeat. I had to be the rock for my wife when she needed me most. And then about a month later — I finally broke down myself.

I've carried a lot of things locked away for a long time. I was raised with the "deal with it and move on" mentality. You don't talk about it. You just push through. And I know I'm not the only one built that way.

That's why I'm building Vault.

Vault is a private app — a judgment-free space where you can say everything you've never been able to say out loud. AI that talks to you like a real friend, not a therapist. Zero data storage. Nobody can read it. Not me. Not anyone. It's yours.

And for veterans — it's free. Forever. No questions asked.

22 veterans die by suicide every day. Not because they're weak. But because the entry point to getting help is completely broken. You're supposed to sit down with a complete stranger and immediately be vulnerable? Most people — especially people built the way we're built — will never do that.

Vault fixes the entry point. You journal privately first. You build trust. Then — only if you want to — it can help connect you with someone. On your terms. At your pace.

This is bigger than an app. The long term vision is physical compounds in all 50 states. Real homes for displaced veterans and homeless individuals. Schools. Clinics. Mental health support. Security staffed by veterans, for veterans. Communities built with dignity.

But it starts here. With this app. With this post.

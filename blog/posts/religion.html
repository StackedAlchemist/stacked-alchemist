There are some things that really get to me. I don't understand why faith takes such a central role when it comes to believing in a higher power — and yet, people speak of the divine words of their deities and how we should all follow in their footsteps to be better people. What I don't understand is this: if someone does not believe the same as you, or sees things differently, what gives you the right to shun them in any way? Does that make you anything like the deity you claim to follow?

Everything I state here is a matter of opinion. I don't know what is going on in the minds of everyone out there — but if you want to be like your deity and follow the example they set, we need to love each other and respect each other's beliefs.

I have had many conversations with many different people who hold many different beliefs. I myself believe there is a higher power that has created us. But there is something I would love people to think about.

We always hear the phrase: "We were created in his image." Here's what I ask you to consider — go create something. Draw it, build it, write it. Whatever it is, just make it. Now look at what you've created. That thing was formed in your image — an image that existed in your mind before it took shape in the world. Does that mean you are that thing? Or that the thing is you?

I would say no — unless you specifically made it look exactly like yourself.

If you believe God created everything, then he created all the animals, all the organisms, all the plants. Does that mean the deity looks like all of these things? I would think not.

Use your holy books as guides to becoming a better person — not as weapons to force down someone's throat. Use them as tools to heal, if a person is willing to accept that healing. Use the words as encouragement for your fellow human if they are willing to listen.

But never judge someone simply because they are not you, and do not think the way that you do.

Be kind and respectful regardless of what you believe — and others will follow.

Some cannot be saved. Evil lays within all of us, and some may succumb to it with no way back. All you can do is wait for the moment they are willing to walk toward the light of your deity — and when that moment comes, be there to lead them through.

That is the only weapon worth carrying.
